promotional teflon kittenbasket is a basic, minimalistic theme to help you get a jump start on your website.

A promotional group of kittens in a basket. The basket may or may not be made of teflon.

I may reuse it for my website or something.

— Mel Chua, #teachingopensource

ptkb was developed by Ian Weller of Red Hat’s Community Architecture and Leadership Team for their websites in May 2011. We’re pretty certain ptkb:

  • presents content cleanly without getting in the way (and without hurting viewers’ eyes)
  • emphasizes already-emphasized content with subtle yellow highlights
  • is totally flexible for any type of design
  • is not known to the State of California to cause cancer

We’ll be working on themes for popular open-source web systems (including WordPress and MediaWiki) in the very near future.

Quick start

Download ptkb-base.css, stick it somewhere accessible, and start with this page:

<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
  <head>
    <title>promotional teflon kittenbasket</title>
    <meta charset="utf-8" />
    <link href="http://fonts.googleapis.com/css?family=Droid+Sans:regular,bold" rel="stylesheet" type="text/css">
    <link rel="stylesheet" href="path/to/ptkb-base.css" />
    <link rel="stylesheet" href="path/to/your.css" />
  </head>
  <body>
    <div id="everything">
      <h2>Hello, world!<h2>
    </div>
  </body>
</html>

Header

Place this at the top of your page, inside #everything. 

<div id="header">
  <h1><a href="#"><span class="textalt">logo alt text</span></a></h1>
  <ul id="nav">
    <li class="active"><a href="1.html">Page 1</a></li>
    <li><a href="2.html">Page 2</a></li>
  </ul>
  <div class="clear"></div>
</div>

Footer

Place this at the bottom of your page, inside #everything.
